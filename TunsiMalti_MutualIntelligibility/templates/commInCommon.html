Phrases between Tunisian Arabic and Maltese

Helow! Kif int illum? X'taghmel illum wara ix-xoghol (il-hidma)?
Labas! Kayf? int (Chniyya 7walik) lyum? Chniyya ta3mil ba3d mil-xidma?
How are you today? What are you doing after work?

Ma naghmelx xejn, ghandek zmien biex tilqani? (Malti doesn't have a "hatta", "xi" - "some" morphed into "xejn") Ma na3mil 7atta chay, 3andik zman bach talqani? I'm not doing anything, do you have some time to meet (or find) me?

Iva, ahna nilquna aktar tard (bil-meher, "meher" - "delay, procrastinate") Aywa, a7na nalquna aktar mmaxxir Yes, I do, we will meet later

Il-boghod tal-bini taqrib kilometer ghad-dar L-banya b3id taqrib kilumatr 3ad-dar The building is about 1km from the house (Malti - "the building's distance")

Basic phrases

Xienu ismek? (“X'jismek?” is everyday Maltese) Chnuwwa ismik? What's your name?


Ismi (Jisimni) [name] Ismi [name] My name is...


Jiena marid/a Ana mrid/a I am sick.


Jiena **migrah/a (**migrah is "covered in ulcers" - mwegga/gggha is injured, ningerah - I was injured, gerahni/gerhuni - he/they injured me Ana majru7/a I am injured


htiegi/htiegajja b-tabib (htiega is more common, and possibly "hieg") 7ajti b-tbib I need a doctor.


Dealing with money/currency

Taqbel il-hlas bid-dollar/[currency] Tiqbil il-xlas bi-...[currency] Do you accept [currency type]?


Tista tbiddelli il-flus? Tnajjam tbaddalli il-flus? Can you change money for me?


Fejn hu ATM? (based on the Tunsi, this would be "magna il-flus tal-banka") Win makinat il-flus mta3 il-banka (money machine of the bank) Where is the ATM?


Kemm tibdil il-flus? (There is no "qaddax" in Maltese) B-qaddash tabdil il-flus? What is the exchange rate?/How much to change money/currencies?


Health

Timxi ma' riglejnek u saqajnek Timchi m3a rijlinek u saqinek You walk with your legs and feet.


Itniffes hafna biex ma titghaxxex Itnaffis bezzaf bach ma titGHachchacha Breathe a lot to not lose energy.


Jqass il-lehgahu kul gimgha YiqaSS l-la7jahu kul jim3a He cuts his beard every week.


In-nies torox ma jistawx jismu' In-nas Trush ma ynajjmuch yasm3u Deaf people can't hear.


In-nies ghomja ma jistawx jaraw xejn In-nas 3imyan ma ynajjmu jchufu hatta chay Blind people can't see anything.


Fl-ghajnejnek fihom xfar, u hwiegeb Fl-3ininik, fihum shafrat, u hwajib The eyes have eyelashes and eyebrows.


Darbu l-miskin bi-ponnijiethom DHrabu l-miskin bi-punyathum They hit the poor boys with their fists.


Household

X’qisien huma ghaz-zokkor? Ch’qyasat hum 3as-sukur What are the measurements for the sugar?


Orbot il-hajta fix-xibka bil-labra UrbuT il-xiT fich-chabka bil-ibra Tie the string into the net with a needle.


Raxx il-gnien b-ilma Rach il-jnina bil-ma Spray the garden with water.


Is-saffajja hi mghobbija bl-ikel IS-Suffaya hi m3ibbya bil-makla The strainer is full of food.


Tikinsu iz-zibel mill-qiegh tal-matbah/kcina Tuknusu iz-zibla mill-qa3a tal-kujina You all sweep the trash from the kitchen floor.


Government/Legal Il-hakem il-gdid tkellel illum u jahkem hawn issa L-7akim il-jdid tkellil il-yum u yu7kum huni tawwa The new ruler was crowned today and now governs here.


Xhedt il-kell jitkellel fuq il-hakem fil-mdina (belt) illum ("Mdina" is a specific place in Malta, "belt" is now "city") Chhidt il-klil yitkillil fuq il-7akim fil-mdina ilyum I witnessed the crown being put on the ruler in the city today.


Tahqar il-gensna kuljum Ta7qir il-jinsna kulyum ("gens" also more commonly means "gender/sex" in both languages) She opresses/tyrannizes our people/nation (race) every day.


Il-pulizja jirkeb il-habsijiet ("jirkeb" is "to make subservient") L-bulis yarqib il-7busat Police control the prisons.


Nature/Environment

Ir-ramel mill-bahar saddad ir-rsas. (‘rsas’ is ‘the act of squeezing’ in Malti) Ir-rmal mill-b7ar Saddid ir-rSaS. The sand from the ocean rusted the fuse.


Waqt ix-xemx ghotob, rajt delli Waqtilli ich-chams GHrub, chuft DHuli When the sun set, I saw my shadow.


Il-mewgiet serqu il-fidda. L-mwaj sriqu l-fuDHDHa The waves stole the silver.


Fit-telgha, xi haga jidahhan sadd in-nadir is-sema FiT-Tal3a, haja yidaxxin sad in-naDHra is-sma. On the hill, something smoking was blocking the view of the sky.


Il-wied jaghmel xi xrariet (“wied” is “torrent”, similar to “stream”) L-wad ya3mil chrarat Streams (of water) make sparks.


Zift jistaghmel biex jmedd it-toroq Zift jista3mil bach yitmad iT-Turuq Tar is used to lay down roads.


Ma tmissx ix-xwiek, sar tdorrok Ma tmisch ich-chukat, sar tDHuruk Don’t touch the thorns, it will hurt you.


Ir-rwejjah jinfhu il-werqtiet tas-sgar fix-xatt Ir-rya7 yunfxuu l-awraq tach-chjar fich-chatt The wind blows the leaves of the trees onto the beach.


Is-sgar ghandhom ferghat ma’ weraq Ich-chjar 3andhum furu3 m3a awraq Trees have branches with leaves.


People

Ghandna sitta ahwa li xxiehru (“xxieher” - “to become famous”) 3andna sitta axwa chkun chuhra We have six brothers who are famous (celebrities).


Il-kettieba kiteb ghaxra kotob Il-katba ktib 3achra kutub The writer wrote ten books.


Il-hawwief jimxi fil-ghabra L-xawwaf yimchi fil-GHabra The wanderer/coward walks through dust.


Ir-ragel u martu ghandhom tliet bniet Ir-rajil u martu 3andhum tlath(a) bnat The man and his wife have three daughters.


L-ghalliem ghallem lit-tlaleb kif jitlobu il-flus L-mu3allim 3allim liT-Tullab kifach yuTlub il-flus The teacher taught the beggars how to ask for money.


Illum l-ghid milied taz-zajjarin (“milied” is only used for “Christmas” even though the common Maltese speaker knows that “milied” technically means “birthday” too) L-yum l-3ad milad taz-zuwwar Today is the traveler’s birthdays.


L-omm se jwelled ghat-tarbija gdida fis-sptar L-um bach ywallid 3al-bebe jdid fiS-SbiTar The mother will give birth to a new baby in the hospital.


Il-kbirat jghossu l-ulied fil-milghab L-ikbar y3issu l-wlad fil-mal3ib The adults are watching the kids on the playground (field).


L-ghedewwak ma jsirx hawn illum L-3duwwak ma ysirch hunna il-yum Your enemy will not be here today.


Qualities

Il-ferh mhux merhi Il-farha mahich mirxi Happiness is not limp/loose.


Zghar jew kbir? SGHir walla kbir? Small or large?


Tghallem ilsien gdid, huwa hafif T3allim lsan jdid, huwa xfif Learning a new language, is easy.


It-triq kien tant dejjaq li il-karozzi ma stajthomx jidhohoml it-triq. It-Turuq kan yasir DHiyyiq illi il-karbha ma najjimhumch yidxalhum it-Turuq The roads were so narrow that cars could not enter the street.


Il-bieb jew it-TV hu mahlul? Smajt xi haga (“mahlul” is “loose, slack” in Malti) L-bab walla it-talvza hu ma7lul? Sma3it haja Is the door open or TV on? I heard something.


Dieher li l-ghalliem sfar DHahir illi il-mu3allim Sfar It is apparent that the teacher went pale.


Gildhu safrani, hu mhux bis-sahha (“isfar” means “yellow” in Malti) Jildhu aSfir, hu mahich bis-sa77a His skin is pale/yellow, he is not healthy.


In-nies fqar ma jistawx jixtri xejn In-nas fuqara ma ynajjimch yichri hatta chay Poor people can’t buy anything.


Time

Il-gimgha ghandu sebgha nhar Il-jim3a 3andhu sab3a nhar The week has 7 days.


Marru ghal-knisja kul l-erbgha u l-hadd Maru 3la knisyya kul n7ar l-irba3 u l-a7add They went to church every Wednesday and Sunday.


M’hemmx waqt fil-Hamis Ma thammach waqt fin-n7ar l-ixmis There is not time on Thursday.


In-nies johorgu dejjem fl-ahhar il-gimgha (Malti is more likely to use “il-weekend”.) In-nas yuxurju dima fil-axir ij-jimgha People always go out on weekends.


Jirfdu kuljum ir-rummieniet Yirfdu kulyum ir-rummanat Everyday they step on the pomegranates.


Kemm jdum in-nofsinhar? B-qaddach ydum in-nuSf in-n7ar How long does noon last?


Il-mitghim hu miftuh ghal-24 is-saghat fin-nhar (“ristorant” is “restaurant” in Malti) Il-maT3im hu mahlul 3la-24 is-swaya3 fin-n7ar The restaurant is open 24 hours a day.


Mehher il-hrik sa ghada (“hrik” is “movement” in Malti) Mmaxxir il-harka hatta GHudwa They delayed the activity until tomorrow.


Min-nofs il-lejl sa is-sebh, nistennaw Min-nuSSilil hatta is-sba7, nistannau From midnight until morning/dawn, we wait.


Siegha siegha, nzuru ghal bliet ohrajn (“siegha siegha” means “hour hour” in Malti) Sa3a sa3a, nzuru 3la buldan axar Occasionally, we travel to different countries.
